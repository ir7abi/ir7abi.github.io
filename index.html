About

I’m a Biology major and Chemistry minor at Lamar University, where I am a David J. Beck Fellow, JAMP Scholar, and the sole 2025 recipient of the Texas State University System Regents’ Student Scholar Award — selected from across seven institutions and more than 102,000 students.

My academic life sits at the intersection of medicine, language, and faith. I conduct neurovascular research at Weill Cornell Medicine on retinal OCT angiography as a window into ischemic stroke, write essays for Pulse and Cadenza literary magazines, and serve as Assistant Imam and Youth Director at Al-Ansar Mosque of The Woodlands. These commitments aren’t separate. Each one teaches me something the others can’t.

Whether I’m scoring neuropsych assessments, leading Fajr at six in the morning, tutoring math, or presenting at an APSA regional meeting — I’m drawn to work that asks for both rigor and care.
